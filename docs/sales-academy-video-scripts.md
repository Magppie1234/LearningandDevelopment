# Sales Academy — Colossyan Video Scripts (Modules 1–8, 10)

Production-ready scripts for **Colossyan** (colossyan.com). One section per
module; each scene = **VO** (avatar voiceover) + **ON SCREEN** (text/graphic
cue). Modules 9 and 11 are `[SOURCE PENDING: Notion]` and have no scripts yet.

**How to produce in Colossyan:** New video → pick one presenter (same avatar
across all modules for continuity) → one Colossyan *scene* per scene below →
paste the VO into the script box → add the ON SCREEN elements as text/shape
layers (or Colossyan's chart/table media) → brand colours: charcoal `#2B2420`,
ivory `#F5EFE6`, copper `#B8703F`; sage `#7C8B6F` for positives, amber
`#e0a04a` for warnings. Target pace ~140 wpm; add a beat (0.5s pause) where
marked `[PAUSE]`.

**Language rules baked in (do not edit back in):** never "carcinogen",
"cheap/discount/negotiate", "yearly deep cleaning", "wooden kitchens are bad",
or "artificial stone" alone; "wonderful" at most once per video.

**Unresolved flags:** scripts never voice a payment split
(`[CONFIRM PAYMENT SPLIT]`), never voice the first-kitchen year
(`[CONFIRM YEAR]`), and never mention the Red Dot award (`[VERIFY]`).

**Scene 2 · The mission**
- VO: "Our mission is to transform ordinary homes into wellness homes. Spaces that keep you, your family, and the planet safe. [PAUSE] Magppie is a Wellness Movement. We don't sell kitchens — we sell health, safety, and twenty-five years of peace of mind."
- ON SCREEN: Mission statement as a full-screen quote card, copper rule above and below.

**Scene 3 · The one story**
- VO: "There is exactly one version of the company story, and it never changes. Magppie Group has been in business for over fifty years. For the past twenty-plus years, we have been creating kitchens and wardrobes. And our first SilverStone kitchen gave us nine-plus years of real-world performance validation."

**Scene 6 · Close**

## Module 2 — SilverStone: The Material Science (~3 min)

**Scene 1 · Hook**
- VO: "It looks like a stone. But it does not behave like a regular stone. This is SilverStone — and this module makes you fluent in it."
- ON SCREEN: Title card: *SilverStone — The Material Science*.

**Scene 2 · What it is**
- VO: "SilverStone is our patented wellness stone. We take porcelain clay, heat it to thirteen hundred degrees Celsius, and infuse it with silver and copper nano-particles. That makes it anti-bacterial, anti-fungal, non-porous, stain-proof, scratch-resistant, and impact-resistant. It's one hundred percent food-grade — you can eat directly off it. Stronger than granite. More elegant than marble. Engineered, not mined."
- ON SCREEN: Process diagram: porcelain clay → 1,300°C kiln → Ag/Cu nano-particles → SilverStone slab.

**Scene 3 · The 7 Safety Pillars (comparison graphic)**
- VO: "Present the seven safety pillars in this order, every time. Stain safe. Scratch safe. High load bearing — drawers hold up to sixty kilos each. Fire safe. Water safe — in a thirty-day water test, wood swelled while the stone stayed unchanged. Impact safe — a heavy ceramic jar dropped on it left it intact. And more storage — up to sixty-two percent more than standard kitchens, sized for large Indian plates."
- ON SCREEN: Animated 7-row table, one pillar revealed per beat, numbers highlighted in copper (60 kg / 30 days / 62%).

**Scene 4 · Versus granite, marble, tiles (comparison graphic)**
- VO: "Granite is porous — it absorbs stains and bacteria and needs polishing. Marble comes from mining and needs constant care. Tiles trap dirt and fungus in their grout lines. SilverStone is non-porous, antibacterial, uniform — with no grout lines at all."
- ON SCREEN: 4-column comparison table: Granite / Marble / Tiles / SilverStone, red ✗ vs sage ✓ per row (porosity, bacteria, maintenance, grout).

**Scene 5 · Versus wood (the money row)**
- VO: "And the big one — compressed wood. The material costs under one hundred rupees a square foot and brings formaldehyde, termites, and moisture damage. SilverStone material is around five hundred rupees a square foot — antibacterial, termite-proof, maintenance-free — at a similar finished kitchen price."
- ON SCREEN: Two bars: wood < ₹100/sq.ft vs SilverStone ~₹500/sq.ft; caption "similar finished price — completely different material".

**Scene 6 · Technical rapid-fire**
- VO: "Know these cold. The cabinet structure balances the stone's weight — no impact on flooring. It won't warp, bend, or sag. The hardware is patented, built in the same European facilities as Blum and Grass, carries over a hundred kilos, and is rust-resistant with a ten-year warranty. And maintenance? A damp cloth. Nothing else, ever."
- ON SCREEN: Icon grid: weight ⚖, warp 🚫, hardware ⚙ (100+ kg, 10 yr), cloth 🧽.

**Scene 7 · Close**
- VO: "Stronger than granite, more elegant than marble, safer than wood. Now prove it — take the quiz."
- ON SCREEN: "Next: the quiz · Pass mark 80%".

## Module 4 — The Consultative Sales Pitch (~3 min)

**Scene 1 · Hook**
- VO: "One flow. Two channels. Whether you're on a call or standing in the showroom, the logic of a Magppie pitch never changes."
- ON SCREEN: Title card: *The Consultative Sales Pitch*.

**Scene 2 · The 8 stages (flowchart)**
- VO: "Eight stages. Open. Discover. Agitate the real problem. Introduce the solution. Go deep on the product. Qualify the budget — before any number. Price with confidence. And close on a concrete next step."
- ON SCREEN: Animated 8-node horizontal flowchart, nodes lighting up in sequence: Opening → Discovery → Problem → Solution → Deep dive → Budget → Pricing → Next steps.

**Scene 3 · Opening & discovery**
- VO: "The opening earns two minutes: introduce yourself, confirm it's a good time — then pause. Genuinely pause. Then discover three things: their city, for serviceability. Own home or investment, for budget context. And whether a designer is involved, for coordination."
- ON SCREEN: Checklist: ⏸ 2-second pause · 📍 City · 🏠 Own vs investment · 📐 Designer?

**Scene 4 · Problem agitation (the heart)**
- VO: "Then the truth most customers have never heard: most regular wooden kitchens have hidden issues — termites, mould, sagging. And the one nobody can see: formaldehyde gas from plywood and M-D-F. The World Health Organisation says formaldehyde can be very harmful for your health — reports link it to cancer. [PAUSE] Deliver that slowly. It matters."
- ON SCREEN: Kitchen cross-section: hidden icons revealed (termites/mould/gas); WHO framing quote card.

**Scene 5 · Budget before price**
- VO: "Never quote before qualifying. Ask: are you looking at a premium wellness solution, or comparing with basic carpentry? Magppie is not in the carpentry segment — say it kindly, and say it early."
- ON SCREEN: Fork graphic: "Premium wellness" → proceed to pricing | "Basic carpentry" → value conversation first.

**Scene 6 · Price and next step**
- VO: "Then the range: eighty-four hundred to ten thousand eight hundred rupees per square foot, by finish. A ten-by-ten kitchen typically lands between twelve and fifteen lakhs. Check alignment — 'does this range fit what you're considering?' — then close on the layout: 'share your kitchen layout, and we'll prepare a customised estimate on WhatsApp.'"
- ON SCREEN: Price band ₹8,400–10,800/sq.ft · 10x10 ≈ ₹12–15 L → WhatsApp CTA. (No payment split shown — [CONFIRM PAYMENT SPLIT] unresolved; the consultant confirms the schedule later.)

**Scene 7 · The showroom variant**
- VO: "In the showroom the same logic wears different clothes: re-qualify the visitor, reframe the category, walk the material science live, justify the premium, frame the fifteen-year value — then pricing, terms, and closure intent."
- ON SCREEN: Vertical 8-step showroom list, tagged "Sales Manager Script".

**Scene 8 · Close**
- VO: "Same flow, every channel. Take the quiz."
- ON SCREEN: "Next: the quiz · Pass mark 80%".

## Module 5 — Objection Handling (~3 min)

**Scene 1 · Hook**
- VO: "An objection isn't a no. It's a request for a better answer. Here are the seven you'll hear most — and exactly how to answer them."
- ON SCREEN: Title card: *Objection Handling* · 7 cards fanned out.

**Scene 2 · "Too expensive"**
- VO: "Compare materials, then lifetimes. Compressed wood costs under a hundred rupees a square foot; SilverStone is around five hundred — for the material alone. Wood kitchens fight termites, water, fungus, and often need replacing within five to seven years. Stone stays as good as new for decades. On lifetime cost, Magppie is usually the smarter investment — with a twenty-five-year guarantee and twenty-five annual services on top."
- ON SCREEN: Split bar: ₹100 vs ₹500 material → lifetime line: wood replaced at yr 5–7 vs stone flat for 25 yrs.

**Scene 3 · "I need to think about it"**
- VO: "Never push. Equip. Offer a short SilverStone video and installation photos on WhatsApp, a quick expert video call, or a sample sent home. Then let them choose: 'which would work better for you?'"
- ON SCREEN: Three offer cards: 🎬 video · 🎥 call · 📦 sample.

**Scene 4 · "I already have a designer"**
- VO: "Perfect — welcome it. We collaborate with designers constantly: technical drawings, 3-D renders, seamless coordination. Many designers recommend us because SilverStone raises the value of their projects. Offer to connect with them directly."
- ON SCREEN: Handshake graphic: Magppie + Designer → shared drawings/renders.

**Scene 5 · "No showroom here" and "Never heard of you"**
- VO: "No showroom? Systems build trust: central manufacturing, trained in-house installers, written commitments, pan-India support — plus a sample, a call, or a site visit. Never heard of Magppie? We're a premium brand — we don't mass-market. Fifty-plus years of heritage, trusted by the Ambanis, Dhoni, Ranbir Kapoor, and a global innovation award at KBIS twenty-twenty-six."
- ON SCREEN: Two cards side by side, each with its 3-beat answer.

**Scene 6 · The technical two**
- VO: "Why not granite or marble? Mining harms the environment, and natural stone is porous — it stains, harbours bacteria, and needs polishing. SilverStone is non-porous and uniform. Too heavy? The cabinet structure distributes the load — no flooring impact, and smoother operation on patented hardware. Brittle? Stronger than granite — the ceramic-jar drop test left it intact."
- ON SCREEN: Three mini-panels: 🌍 mining ✗ / porous ✗ · ⚖ load distributed ✓ · 🏺 jar-drop test ✓.

**Scene 7 · Close**
- VO: "Seven objections, seven confident answers. Quiz time."
- ON SCREEN: "Next: the quiz · Pass mark 80%".

- VO: "Price with total confidence — because everything about Magppie pricing is fixed, transparent, and defensible."

**Scene 3 · Included vs separate**
- VO: "Included: SilverStone cabinets and shutters, internal shelves, soft-close hardware, factory fabrication, transport, and installation. Quoted separately: accessories, appliances, premium upgrades. And the customer's scope — electrical points, plumbing, civil changes — goes into a written scope matrix before confirmation. **Scene 4 · Guarantees**
- VO: "The guarantees do the selling: twenty-five years, unconditional, on the stone — termites, water, discoloration, swelling, warping. Ten years on hardware. Two on lighting. And twenty-five complimentary annual services — deep cleaning, sanitisation, alignment."

**Scene 6 · Payment terms — say this carefully**

**Scene 7 · Close**

- ON SCREEN: "Next: the quiz · Pass mark 80%".

## Module 7 — Approved Language & Messaging Standards (~2.5 min)

**Scene 1 · Hook**
- VO: "Every rule in this module came from a real customer call that went wrong. This is how Magppie sounds — on every channel."
- ON SCREEN: Title card: *Approved Language & Messaging Standards*.

**Scene 2 · The forbidden words (comparison graphic)**
- VO: "Seven swaps, non-negotiable. Never 'carcinogen' — say it can be very harmful for your health, and that reports link it to cancer. 'Wonderful' — once per conversation, then vary it. Never 'yearly deep cleaning' — it's twenty-five complimentary annual services. Never 'wooden kitchens are bad' — most regular wooden kitchens have hidden issues. Never 'artificial stone' alone — engineered stone, or our own patented stone. Never cheap, discount, or negotiate — fixed price policy, complete transparency. And never 'I don't know' — let me check with our team and get back to you."
- ON SCREEN: Animated two-column table, one row per beat: ❌ term → ✅ replacement.

**Scene 3 · Statements into questions**
- VO: "Flat statements lose people. Convert them: 'these are the most commonly used materials' becomes 'these are the most commonly used materials, right sir?' Small question, full engagement."
- ON SCREEN: WRONG/RIGHT quote cards morphing.

**Scene 4 · Pacing**
- VO: "And pacing: pause two seconds after asking for their time. One second before any price. One second after a health fact. Keep sentences under eighteen words. One idea per breath. And pronounce it mag-pee — repeat it if they ask."
- ON SCREEN: Rhythm bar: ⏸2s → 💰⏸1s → 🫁 15–18 words · "MAG-PEE".

**Scene 5 · Close**
- VO: "Language is the product before the product arrives. Quiz time."
- ON SCREEN: "Next: the quiz · Pass mark 80%".
